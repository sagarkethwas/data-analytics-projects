Exploratory Data Analysis Project
This exploratory data analysis was conducted on a telecom customer dataset containing 7,043 records and 21 attributes, with the goal of understanding factors driving customer churn.

Churn Rate: Around 26–27% of customers were found to have churned, indicating a significant retention challenge.

Demographics: The gender distribution is roughly equal (50.5% Male, ~49.5% Female), suggesting churn is not gender-specific. Senior Citizens account for about 16% of the customer base but show a higher churn rate (41%), highlighting them as a vulnerable group.

Contract Type: Customers on month-to-month contracts make up 55% of the base and contribute over 85% of churners, while those with one-year or two-year contracts have much lower churn rates (11% and ~3%, respectively).

Internet Service: About 74% of customers have Internet service, with Fiber Optic users showing a much higher churn rate (42%) compared to DSL (18%).

Online Services: Lack of Online Security, Device Protection, and Tech Support strongly correlates with churn – customers without these add-ons are 20–25% more likely to leave.

Charges: Churn is higher among customers with monthly charges above $70, showing price sensitivity in the higher spending segment.

Tenure: Customers with tenure under 12 months contribute ~50% of churn, emphasizing the importance of improving onboarding and early engagement.

Overall, churn is primarily driven by contract type, tenure, and internet service type. The findings suggest that encouraging longer-term contracts, offering security and support add-ons, and targeted retention efforts for new and fiber optic customers could significantly reduce churn.
